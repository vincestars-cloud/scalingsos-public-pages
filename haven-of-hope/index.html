When getting through the day takes too much

You may be capable, caring—and quietly exhausted.

From the outside, things can look fine. Inside, you may be rehearsing every conversation, overextending for everyone else, or wondering why the same fear keeps returning. Therapy creates a place to slow the pattern down and meet it with curiosity instead of judgment.

04

You know the pattern—but cannot think your way out

Insight is present. What is missing is a safe way to practice something new.

Meet your therapist

Warmth without vagueness. Insight with something to do next.

I’m the Haven of Hope therapist, a relational clinician who believes meaningful change begins when you feel understood enough to be honest—and supported enough to experiment.

How sessions may take shape

Integrative care, not a one-size-fits-all script.

Use this section to accurately describe only the modalities the clinician is trained and permitted to offer.

01

Relational therapy

We notice patterns as they arise in real time and use the therapeutic relationship as a place to practice honesty, repair, and trust.

02

Attachment-informed work

We explore how early relationships shaped expectations around closeness, independence, conflict, and emotional safety.

03

Body-aware practices

We include physical cues, pacing, grounding, and nervous-system responses when talking alone is not enough.

A thoughtful fit matters

What this practice is designed for.

A consultation is the best place to discuss your needs, clinical appropriateness, scheduling, and referrals.

This may be a good fit when…

  • You are an adult seeking individual, non-emergency outpatient therapy.
  • You want both insight and practical experimentation.
  • You are open to noticing emotional and body-based responses.
  • You value a collaborative therapist who offers feedback.

A different level of care may help when…

  • You need immediate crisis intervention or 24-hour support.
  • You are seeking medication management or formal testing.
  • You need a service this clinician is not licensed or trained to provide.
  • Your goals would be better served by a specialist or structured program.

Haven of Hope provides non-emergency outpatient therapy. If you are in immediate danger, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

A note from Haven of Hope

I believe people make sense in context.

The habits you are frustrated with may once have helped you stay connected, prepared, accepted, or safe.

That does not mean those habits still have to run the show. In therapy, we can honor what a pattern protected while also asking whether it is costing you too much now.

My style is active and relational. I will listen carefully, name patterns with care, and help us keep sight of what you want to be different outside the therapy room. Some sessions may feel reflective; others may include practicing language, tracking body cues, or slowing down a moment that usually passes too quickly to understand.

I do not expect you to arrive polished. You are welcome to be uncertain, contradictory, angry, hopeful, quiet, or still searching for words. We will start with what is here.

01

Context over judgment

We look for the function beneath a behavior before trying to change it.

02

Collaboration over performance

You do not have to be a “good client.” Feedback and questions are part of the work.

03

Practice over perfection

Change grows through small, repeatable experiences—not flawless insight.

Start booking

The consultation is a brief phone conversation about what is bringing you to therapy, what you hope will change, scheduling needs, and practical questions. It is also a chance for both of us to notice whether the fit feels comfortable. You do not need to share a detailed history.

Yes. Haven of Hope offers in-person sessions in Killeen and virtual appointments for eligible clients. During the consultation, we can talk through which appointment type feels most supportive for your schedule, privacy, and comfort.

Session fees and payment options are shared during your free 15-minute consultation, so you have clear information before scheduling. Haven of Hope accepts major credit cards and HSA/FSA cards.

Insurance and out-of-network options are confirmed during your consultation. If you plan to seek possible reimbursement, ask about monthly superbills when you reach out to Haven of Hope.

Most clients begin weekly so there is enough continuity to build trust and momentum. Some clients move to biweekly sessions once goals are clearer and the work feels more stable.

Haven of Hope asks for at least 48 hours’ notice for cancellations. Late cancellations and missed sessions are billed at the full session fee, with exceptions for emergencies, sudden illness, or unsafe weather conditions.

The first session usually focuses on context, current concerns, strengths, goals, and what helps you feel safe enough to engage. There should be no expectation that you disclose every difficult experience immediately.

There is no universal timeline. Some people seek focused support around a specific concern; others choose longer-term work with layered relationship or trauma patterns. Progress and fit should be reviewed together over time.

Therapy is private, with specific legal and ethical limits. Those limits include immediate safety concerns, suspected abuse or neglect of a child or vulnerable adult, and certain court orders. These details are reviewed before treatment begins.
