Good questions

Things families ask us

How much does a stairlift cost?
Stairlifts can range in price depending on fit, function, whether it's straight or curved, the number of landings and other selected features. Straight stairlifts tend to be more affordable, while curved lifts require custom rails. Scheduling a free consultation helps validate the right product and allows for accurate pricing.
How long does a stairlift take to install, and how quickly can I get it?
Most straight stairlifts can be installed in 2–4 hours. Curved models may take longer due to custom fabrication. We keep stairlifts in stock for prompt installation; custom models take longer and can normally be shipped within three weeks.
Can I install a stairlift myself?
No. Professional installation ensures safety, correct fitting, and warranty coverage.
How do you install the rail? Do you use the walls?
The stairlift rail sits on feet that are installed into the tread of your steps. We do not use the walls for these installations.
How do I know if a stairlift will fit my staircase? Can it handle a 90° or 180° turn?
We offer free in-home consultations to assess your staircase and recommend the best solution. Yes — we offer custom curved stairlifts designed to fit the unique contours of your staircase.
Can it turn?
Yes. The seat rotates at the top of the stairs so it's safer to get on and off. If your staircase has a turn, the stairlift can be custom designed to accommodate it.
How much weight can the stairlift carry?
Our stairlifts have varying weight capacities, typically ranging from 264 lb to 350 lb. Our heavy-duty stairlifts can carry up to 600 lb. Each stairlift is designed for use by one person at a time.
What happens during a power outage?
Our stairlifts include battery backup systems, so they keep operating during power outages.
Do you offer discounts?
Seniors (65+), active service members and veterans qualify for 10% off. We'll also help you explore VA grants, Medicaid waivers and FSA/HSA funds where they apply.
