What we handle

Batteries, alternators, starters, and the "it clicked once and died" problem. We test the whole circuit rather than just swapping the battery and hoping — a bad alternator will kill a brand new one within days.
Pads, rotors, calipers, fluid, and diagnosing squeals, grinding, pulling and a soft pedal. Brake work is one of the most common mobile jobs — it rarely needs a lift, and we'd rather you not drive on it.
We scan the codes, then work out what the codes actually mean. A code points at a circuit, not a part — replacing whatever the reader names is how people end up paying twice. You get told what we found and what it costs before anything is replaced.
Oil and filter, coolant, transmission and brake fluid, belts, hoses, plugs, filters. The routine work that's easiest to put off and most expensive to skip — done on your driveway while you get on with your day.
Overheating, coolant leaks, thermostats, fans and radiators, plus AC that's blowing warm. In Florida the AC isn't a luxury, and an overheating engine is the fastest way to turn a small bill into a large one.
Being straight with you: internal engine and transmission rebuilds, anything needing a lift or alignment rack, and heavy bodywork need a shop. If your job turns out to be one of those, we'll tell you on the spot and point you somewhere rather than take it apart in your driveway.
