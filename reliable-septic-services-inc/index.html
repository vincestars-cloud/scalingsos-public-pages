The process

What actually happens when we pump

No mystery. Here's exactly how a standard visit goes.

STEP 1

Locate the tank

We find and uncover the access lid — or add a riser so the next pump is easy.

STEP 2

Pump it out

Full removal of solids and liquids with our vacuum truck, down to the bottom.

STEP 3

Inspect & photograph

We check baffles, walls, and the outlet, and photograph the condition.

STEP 4

Lid resealed, soil and grass raked back. You'd never know we were there.

Questions

Septic pumping FAQs

How much does septic pumping cost?

It depends on your tank size, how accessible the lid is, and its condition — so we give you the price upfront, before any work begins. Call us and we'll walk you through it; no obligation.

How often should I pump my tank?

For most households, every 3–5 years. Smaller tanks, larger families, or heavy garbage-disposal use push that to the shorter end. We'll give you a personalized interval after we see your tank.

Do I need to find or dig up the tank first?

No. Locating and uncovering the lid is included. If you'd like easier future access, we can install a riser so the lid sits at ground level — optional, and we'll quote it upfront.

Will it make a mess or smell?

There's a brief odor while the lid is open, but it clears quickly. We seal everything back up and rake the soil and grass back into place — your yard is left the way we found it.

Can you do an inspection for a home sale?

Yes. We do point-of-sale pump-and-inspect visits and provide documentation for closing. Let us know your timeline and we'll work around the inspection period.
