Straight answers

No. You choose the repair facility. Your insurer can recommend a shop from their network, and many states have anti-steering rules that specifically bar them from pressuring you into one, but the decision is yours. Bring us the claim and we'll take it from there.
On a covered claim, your deductible — that's it. We invoice your carrier for the balance. If another driver was at fault and their carrier accepts liability, you may owe nothing at all. Self-pay repairs get a written estimate before any work starts.
Bumper and cosmetic work is usually 2–4 days. Moderate collision runs 1–2 weeks. Heavy structural depends on parts availability and how fast the carrier approves the supplement. You get a firm date at approval, and a text at every stage after that.
Whatever your policy covers, and we'll tell you plainly which is which. Many policies default to aftermarket or recycled parts on older vehicles. If you want OEM and your policy won't cover the difference, we'll quote you the gap up front rather than surprising you at pickup.
Yes. If your policy includes rental coverage we'll coordinate it directly so there's no gap between drop-off and pickup. If it doesn't, we'll point you to a rental partner nearby.
Our workmanship — the repair work itself, plus paint adhesion and finish defects — for as long as you own the vehicle. It does not transfer to a new owner, and it does not cover new damage, normal wear, or parts covered by the manufacturer's own warranty. You get the terms in writing at delivery.
